Rajma Chawal

Description

Rajma chawal is a popular North Indian dish that is made very often in Indian households. Here boiled kidney beans are simmered in a thick, spiced onion-tomato gravy. This rajma masala is served with plain rice and sliced onions to make a complete meal.

My Mom’s recipe for restaurant-style Punjabi Rajma Masala is a staple in our home, and after you try it I’ll bet it becomes one at yours, too. So what are we waiting for, let's get straight into it!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup kidney beans) – about 200 grams rajma, soaked overnight in enough water
  • 1/2 to 1 teaspoon chopped green chillies or 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon chopped garlic or 7 to 8 medium-sized garlic
  • 1 tablespoon chopped ginger or 2 inches chopped ginger
  • 3 medium tomato, chopped – about 200 grams
  • 1 large onion, chopped – about 100 grams
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder or cayenne pepper
  • 3/4 to 1 teaspoon Garam Masala Powder
  • 1 teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur powder)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoon butter or oil
  • salt as required

Instructions

  1. Soak 1 cup (200 grams) kidney beans in enough water overnight or for 8 to 9 hours.
  2. Drain all the water and set the beans aside.
  3. Finely chop the vegetables– 1 large onion (100 gms), 3 medium tomatoes (200 gms), 1 inch ginger, 7 to 8 medium sized garlic and 1 green chilli. Keep aside.
  4. In a pressure cooker add the kidney beans together with the chopped onions, tomatoes, ginger, garlic and green chilies.
  5. Now add spice, water and mix well
  6. Add butter/oil
  7. Let it cook for 18-20 mins. Mix well and simmer till the gravy consistency thickens. Mash a few kidney beans with the back of a spoon to thicken the consistency.
  8. That's it your rajma gravy is prepared.

Enjoy it with rice !!
